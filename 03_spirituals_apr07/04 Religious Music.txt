CONTENT:
“I have sometimes thought that the mere hearing of those songs would do more to impress some minds with the horrible character of slavery, than the reading of whole volumes of philosophy on the subject could do… To those songs I trace my first glimmering conception of the dehumanizing character of slavery… Those songs still follow me, to deepen my hatred of slavery, and quicken my sympathies for my brethren in bonds…”

=== SLIDE 5 ===
TITLE: What do we know about early religious music?

CONTENT:
First Great Awakening of 1740s largely bypassed enslaved population because of language barrier, resistance among enslaved who preferred African religion and resistance among white enslavers, esp. around egalitarianism of Christianity
Early descriptions of music the will become spirituals are often “critical”: substandard poetry, new compositions, fear of influence on white music

=== SLIDE 16 ===
TITLE: Transitional Gospel

CONTENT:
[Charles Albert] Tindley Style: gospel hymn with instrumental accompaniment (piano and organ), verse-chorus external structure, incorporates call-response, blue 3rds and 7ths, improvisation in performance
Rural gospel style: close to blues, guitar, harmonica, simple changes
Holiness-Pentecostal style (Church of God in Christ): closest to spirituals, singing, clapping, shouting, call-response, heterophony, trombones, trumpets, mandolins, jugs, celebratory, some of the same repertoire as spirituals    Arizona Juanita Dranes: https://youtu.be/sDlfPoMD8Pc
